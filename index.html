Internet Services Terminology

This section is to help clarify some of the terms used by internet service providers to describe the packages and internet services they provide. In other words, this section is dedicated to the user specifically - No one wants to pay for they don't even understand

Terms

Explanation

Capped

The total amount of data you are able to download and upload will be limited. If this limit is reached during the term (Always a monthly period), your internet will be suspended until the start of the new period.

Uncapped

The total amount of data you are able to download and upload will not be limited, however your data could be subject to a fair usage policy. There is no point of internet usage where your internet will be turned off due to data usage.

Fair Usage

Fair usage is a soft cap placed on uncapped internet packages. Once this data usage has been reached within the month, your internet will be throttled, but still on - Thus your internet will be slower, but not offline.

Throttled

Once an uncapped internet line has reached its fair usage policy soft cap, the line wil be slowed by around half of its normal bandwidth, meaning that its speed will decrease to about half of its normal speed.
